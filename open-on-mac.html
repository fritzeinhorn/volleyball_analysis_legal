🍎 Opening VolleyRecon on macOS

VolleyRecon is not yet distributed through the Mac App Store. The app is delivered as a direct download from iCloud Drive. Because macOS checks that apps come from identified developers, you need to follow a few extra steps to open it for the first time.

Step 1 – Download the App

Download the macOS version of VolleyRecon from the link below:

☁️ Open iCloud Download Page
  1. Click the link above to open the iCloud Drive page in your browser.
  2. Click the Download button (cloud icon ↓) next to the macOS app file.
  3. Once the download is complete, open your Downloads folder in Finder.
  4. If the file is a .zip archive, double-click it to extract the .app bundle.
  5. Drag VolleyRecon.app to your Applications folder (optional but recommended).

Step 2 – Bypass the "Unidentified Developer" Warning

When you double-click VolleyRecon for the first time, macOS Gatekeeper may display a message such as:

"VolleyRecon cannot be opened because it is from an unidentified developer."

This is expected for apps not yet listed in the App Store. Use one of the two methods below to open the app anyway.

Method A – Right-click / Control-click (easiest)

  1. In Finder, locate VolleyRecon.app.
  2. Right-click (or Control-click) on the app icon.
  3. Choose Open from the context menu.
  4. A dialog will appear asking whether you are sure — click Open again.
  5. The app will launch and macOS will remember your choice for future launches.

Method B – System Settings (macOS 13 Ventura and later)

  1. Try to open the app by double-clicking it. The warning dialog will appear — click Done to dismiss it.
  2. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security.
  3. Scroll down to the Security section. You should see a message such as "VolleyRecon was blocked from use because it is not from an identified developer."
  4. Click Open Anyway.
  5. Enter your macOS password if prompted, then click Open Anyway once more in the confirmation dialog.

Method B – System Preferences (macOS 12 Monterey and earlier)

  1. Try to open the app. The warning dialog will appear — click OK to dismiss it.
  2. Open System Preferences → Security & Privacy → General tab.
  3. Near the bottom you will see a message about VolleyRecon being blocked. Click Open Anyway.
  4. Enter your macOS password if prompted, then click Open Anyway in the confirmation dialog.

Step 3 – First Launch

After completing the steps above, VolleyRecon will open normally. On all subsequent launches you can simply double-click the app — no extra steps are needed.

Tip: If you see a dialog asking whether the app can access files in your Downloads or Documents folder, click Allow so that VolleyRecon can open your .dvw scouting files and video files.

Why is this necessary?

macOS requires apps to be signed with an Apple Developer certificate and notarized by Apple before they can be opened without warnings. This process is tied to a paid Apple Developer Program membership. VolleyRecon will be submitted to the App Store in the future, which will remove the need for these extra steps.

The app itself is safe — it processes all data locally on your device and does not send any information to external servers.
